Bike Sales Dashboard – Project Explanation

This dashboard was created in Microsoft Excel to analyze customer characteristics and identify the factors that influence bike purchases. The goal of the dashboard is to provide a clear and interactive view of customer behavior using slicers and visual charts so that business users can easily filter data and make decisions.

The dashboard includes filters for marital status, region, and education level, which allow the user to dynamically change the analysis and view results for specific customer groups.

Use of Slicers (Interactive Filters)

The dashboard contains slicers for:
Marital Status (Married / Single)
Region (Europe, North America, Pacific)
Education Level (Bachelors, Graduate Degree, High School, Partial College, Partial High School):

These slicers allow the dashboard to update automatically based on the selected category.
For example, a user can view the purchase behavior of married customers in North America with a graduate degree.

This makes the dashboard interactive and useful for business decision-making because it allows quick comparison between different customer groups.

Average Income per Purchase (Gender vs Purchase Decision)
This chart shows the relationship between customer income, gender, and whether the customer purchased a bike.

Key insights:
Female customers who purchased bikes have higher average income than females who did not purchase.
Male customers who purchased bikes have slightly lower income than males who did not purchase.
This suggests that income may influence purchase decisions differently for males and females.

Business insight:
Higher-income female customers are more likely to buy bikes.
Male customers may purchase bikes regardless of income level.

Customer Age Bracket Analysis
This chart compares the number of customers who purchased bikes across different age groups.
Key insights:
Middle-aged customers have the highest number of purchases.
Older customers have the lowest number of purchases.
This indicates that age is an important factor in customer buying behavior.

Business insight:
Middle-aged customers are the most suitable target market.
Older customers are less likely to purchase bikes.
Using slicers, the user can check if this pattern changes across different regions, education levels, or marital status.

Customer Commute Distance Analysis
This chart shows how commute distance affects the likelihood of purchasing a bike.

Key insights:
Customers with short to medium commute distances (0–5 miles) are more likely to buy bikes.
Customers with long commute distances (more than 10 miles) are less likely to buy bikes.
Very long distance commuters may prefer cars or public transport instead of bikes.

Business insight:
Customers with short or moderate travel distance are the best target for bike sales.
Marketing efforts should focus on customers who live close to their workplace.

Overall Findings from the Dashboard
From the analysis, the following patterns were observed:
Income level influences buying behavior, especially among female customers.
Middle-aged customers are more likely to purchase bikes.
Customers with shorter commute distances are more likely to buy bikes.
Older customers have the lowest purchase rate.
The slicers make it possible to analyze specific customer segments quickly.
This type of dashboard can help businesses understand their customers better and make data-driven marketing and sales decisions.

Business Recommendations / Next Steps 

Focus on middle-aged customers :
They have the highest purchase rate, so marketing should target this age group.

Target short-distance commuters (0–5 miles):
Customers with shorter commutes are more likely to buy bikes. Promote bikes as a convenient daily transport option.

Use income-based pricing strategy:
Higher-income females show higher purchase rates. Offer premium options for high-income customers and affordable options for others.

Apply regional marketing strategy:
Use the region slicer insights to increase promotion in high-performing areas and improve campaigns in low-performing regions.

Segment customers by education level:
Tailor marketing messages to specific customer groups instead of using one general campaign.

Focus on the ideal customer profile
Most likely buyer: middle-aged, short-distance commuter, moderate/high income.
Targeting this group can improve sales efficiency.
